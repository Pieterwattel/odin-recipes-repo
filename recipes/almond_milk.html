Almond Milk

How to Make Almond Milk At Home

We hope you LOVE this milk! It’s:

Fresh, creamy, slightly nutty &
Super Delicious

Ingredients

  • 112 g raw almonds (soaked overnight in cool water // or 1-2 hours in very hot water)
  • 1200 ml filtered water (less to thicken, more to thin)
  • 1 pinch sea salt

Steps

  1. Add your soaked almonds, water and salt to a high-speed blender and blend until creamy and smooth. Keep it running for at least 1-2 minutes so you get the most out of your almonds.
  2. Strain using a nut milk bag or my favorite trick – a thin dish towel. Simply lay a clean dish towel over a mixing bowl, pour over the almond milk, carefully gather the corners, and lift up. Then squeeze until all of the liquid is extracted. Discard pulp, or save for adding to baked goods (especially crackers). Find more ways to use almond pulp here.
  3. Transfer milk to a jar or covered bottle and refrigerate. Shake well before drinking, as it tends to separate.
Your almond milk can be stored in the fridge up to 4-5 days. Enjoy!
